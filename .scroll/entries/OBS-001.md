# Small sample artifacts can create false calibration patterns

## Context

During calibration analysis of prediction buckets, the 60-69% confidence range was initially labeled a "danger zone" due to poor accuracy. However, as more predictions accumulated, this pattern disappeared.

## Observation

The 60-69% confidence bucket showed poor calibration (danger zone) with small samples but became perfectly calibrated as sample size increased to 25 predictions (64.0% accuracy at 64.5% expected confidence, p=1.000).

## Significance

This demonstrates that statistical patterns in small samples can be misleading artifacts. The finding reinforces the need for sufficient sample sizes before drawing conclusions about calibration patterns. The "danger zone" label was appropriately removed once adequate data was collected.
